About me:

I am an educator, artist, community organizer, and hacker.

I have been working in the technology field for 4+ years, currently employed in a DevOps / Site Reliability Engineering role, and recently making a foray into MLOps. Previously, I was employed as a Quality Assurance Engineer. While I code primarily in JavaScript, I've also worked on projects in Python as well as Java. The transition to DevOps was fairly evident, as I already enjoyed playing with .yml CI pipelines and Bash scripting. In addition, I've contributed to both frontend (React) and backend (REST/GraphQL) production code, as well as managed releases, and run regular team meetings, as well as doing support work.

I'm always looking for new things to learn, whether tools to improve my productivity at work, or side projects to expand in other areas. I enjoy playing with the linux terminal and learning about application security and penetration testing. In the past three years I've participated in MontreHack and NorthSec both as a learner and as a volunteer. Badge-soldering and key-casting were among my favourite activities at NorthSec 2023, and I'm really excited about the opportunity to play with robots again I've been afforded in my current role, something I hadn't done since CEGEP. I appreciate open source, backward-compatibility and sustainable design in hardware and software, as well as privacy-preserving technologies, and make contributions to such projects when I can. I take pleasure in combining multiple generations of technology (I aspire towards having a setup like that of Adam in Only Lovers Left Alive).

I've made a video game proof-of-concept through the Pixelles Game Incubator. My goal was to use the eight weeks of the Incubator to complete a teaser for a larger project, in which I hoped to incorporate elements of hardware and software hacking. The latest build is available here.

I have a strong teaching background as well, having worked as a teaching assistant for both Introductory Computer Science and Anthropology courses. I have taught a course on basic computer skills to hard-of-hearing individuals. I have also taught English as a Second Language, and have significant experience as a tutor. Courses I have tutored for have included high school math and science, test prep (SAT), and university-level courses on introductory programming, qualitative research methods, and statistics.

I have an M.A. in Social and Cultural Anthropology from Concordia University in Montreal, and a B.Sc. from McGill University (joint major in Computer Science and Biology).
