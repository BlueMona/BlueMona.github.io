About me:

I am an educator, artist, community organizer, and coder.

I have been working in the technology field for 3+ years, primarily employed as a Quality Assurance Engineer, working with tools such as cucumber (BDD), WebdriverIO (Appium and Selenium) and Postman. While I code primarily in JavaScript, I've also worked on projects in Python as well as Java. I particularly enjoy playing with .yml CI pipelines and Bash scripting. In addition, I've contributed to both frontend (React) and backend (REST/GraphQL) production code, and have recently also begun managing releases on my team at Rally.

I'm always looking for new things to learn, whether tools to improve my productivity at work, or side projects to expand in other areas. I enjoy playing with the linux terminal and learning about application security and penetration testing. In the past two years I've participated in MontreHack and NorthSec both as a learner and as a volunteer. I appreciate open source, backward-compatibility and sustainable design in hardware and software, as well as privacy-preserving technologies, and make contributions to such projects when I can. I take pleasure in combining multiple generations of technology (I aspire towards having a setup like that of Adam in Only Lovers Left Alive).

I'm currently working on my first video game through the Pixelles Game Incubator. I am aiming to use the eight weeks of the Incubator to complete a teaser for a larger project, in which I hope to incorporate elements of hardware and software hacking.

In the past, I have worked as a teaching assistant for both Introductory Computer Science and Anthropology courses. I have taught a course on basic computer skills to hard-of-hearing individuals. I have also taught English as a Second Language, and have significant experience as a tutor. Courses I have tutored for have included high school math and science, test prep (SAT), and university-level courses on introductory programming, qualitative research methods, and statistics.

I have an M.A. in Social and Cultural Anthropology from Concordia University in Montreal, and a B.Sc. from McGill University (joint major in Computer Science and Biology).
